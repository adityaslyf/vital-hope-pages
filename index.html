what is cancer?

Cancer refers to a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells in the body.Normally, cells in the body grow, divide, and die in an orderly manner. However, cancer cells do not follow this normal pattern and instead continue to grow and divide uncontrollably, often forming tumors or invading nearby tissues and organs.

There are many different types of cancer, each with its own set of symptoms, causes, and treatment options. Some common types of cancer include lung cancer, breast cancer, prostate cancer, skin cancer, and colorectal cancer.

The causes of cancer are varied and complex, but many factors can increase a person's risk of developing the disease, including genetics, exposure to certain chemicals or substances, unhealthy lifestyle habits such as smoking or excessive alcohol consumption, and certain infections such as human papillomavirus (HPV) or hepatitis B and C.

Cancer can start almost anywhere in the human body, which is made up of trillions of cells. Normally, human cells grow and multiply (through a process called cell division) to form new cells as the body needs them. When cells grow old or become damaged, they die, and new cells take their place.

how does cancer develope?

Cancer develops when the normal processes that regulate the growth and division of cells are disrupted, causing cells to grow and divide uncontrollably. This can happen due to changes, or mutations, in the DNA of the cells.


Cancer develops when the normal processes that regulate the growth and division of cells are disrupted, causing cells to grow and divide uncontrollably. This can happen due to changes, or mutations, in the DNA of the cells. Normally, the body carefully controls the growth and division of cells to ensure that old or damaged cells are replaced with new ones. But when mutations occur in certain genes that control cell growth and division, the affected cells can start to divide and grow in an uncontrolled manner.


These mutated cells can form a mass of tissue called a tumor. Not all tumors are cancerous; benign tumors are non-cancerous and usually do not pose a significant health risk. However, cancerous tumors, also known as malignant tumors, can invade nearby tissues and organs, and spread to other parts of the body through the bloodstream or lymphatic system.


The exact causes of mutations that lead to cancer are not fully understood, but certain risk factors can increase a person's likelihood of developing the disease. These can include genetic predisposition, exposure to certain chemicals or substances, lifestyle factors such as smoking and poor diet, and age.

Types of cancer?

  • Breast cancer: This is a type of cancer that develops in the breast tissue, usually in women.
  • Lung cancer: This type of cancer typically develops in the lungs, usually as a result of smoking or exposure to certain environmental toxins.
  • Prostate cancer: This type of cancer affects the prostate gland in men.
  • Colorectal cancer: This type of cancer affects the colon or rectum, which are parts of the digestive system.
  • Skin cancer: There are several types of skin cancer, including melanoma, basal cell carcinoma, and squamous cell carcinoma.
  • Leukemia: This is a type of cancer that affects the blood and bone marrow.
  • Lymphoma: This is a type of cancer that affects the lymphatic system, which is part of the immune system.
  • Pancreatic cancer: This is a type of cancer that develops in the pancreas, an organ in the digestive system.
  • Ovarian cancer: This is a type of cancer that affects the ovaries in women.
  • Brain cancer: This type of cancer affects the brain and can be very difficult to treat.

symptoms

The symptoms of cancer can vary depending on the type and stage of the disease, but some common symptoms may include:


  1. Unexplained weight loss
  2. Fatigue and weakness
  3. Pain, particularly if it persists for more than a few weeks
  4. Changes in the skin, such as moles that change size or shape, or new spots or bumps
  5. Persistent cough or hoarseness
  6. Difficulty swallowing or persistent indigestion
  7. Changes in bowel or bladder habits
  8. Unusual bleeding or discharge, such as blood in urine, stool or coughing up blood, abnormal vaginal bleeding or discharge
  9. Enlarged lymph nodes or swelling in parts of the body
  10. Unexplained fever or night sweats.

Prevention Methods

Here are some general ways to prevent cancer:


  1. Avoid smoking and using tobacco products.
  2. Limit alcohol consumption.
  3. Maintain a healthy weight and avoid obesity
  4. Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
  5. Stay physically active and exercise regularly.
  6. Protect your skin from the sun by wearing protective clothing and using sunscreen.
  7. Get vaccinated against viruses that can cause cancer, such as human papillomavirus (HPV) and hepatitis B.
  8. Practice safe sex and use condoms to reduce the risk of sexually transmitted infections that can cause cancer.
  9. Get regular check-ups and screenings for cancer, especially if you have a family history of the disease.
  10. Minimize exposure to environmental toxins and carcinogens, such as asbestos, radon, and industrial chemicals.

NGOs helping cancer patients facing monetary crisis for treatment

organisation

Cuddles Foundation

This child-focused cancer care NGO believes that providing nutrition to the illness-affected, especially children, is one of the most effective ways of fighting cancer. Around 40% of children with cancer in India are malnourished at diagnosis. Cuddles believes in providing holistic nutrition to underprivileged children fighting cancer. Cuddles Foundation - Cancer Care NGO for children

NGO

indian cancer society

Founded in 1951, Indian Cancer Society is the first NGO which started helping cancer patients in India. Established with the intention of creating awareness, detection and cure for cancer patients across the country, they have been continuously extending their support to many underprivileged cancer patients ever since their inception.

cancer society

American Cancer Society

The American Cancer Society (ACS) is a nationwide, community-based voluntary health organization that offers a variety of services to patients and their families. Headquartered in Atlanta, the ACS has state divisions and more than 3,400 local offices.

charitable institutions

HelpAge india

HelpAge India has a special unit for cancer care, through which they have been supporting elderly cancer patients since 1998. So far they have supported the treatment of nearly 99,000 patients. In association with several organizations and cancer hospitals, this NGO for cancer patients in India also organizes cancer awareness and cancer detection programmes.
